### High — Judge is not answer-blind

The judge prompt explicitly receives `expected_behavior` and `forbidden_behavior`, including the exact expected findings. This does not appear leaked into the agent-arm prompts, but the judge is validating against a supplied answer key rather than independently determining task correctness.

### Medium — No enforced network isolation

Run prompts say not to access the network, and no network/push commands were observed. However, the runner only uses a read-only sandbox and prompt instruction; the judge prompt does not include an explicit no-network instruction. Therefore “all sessions used explicit no-network prompts” is unsupported.

Evidence: [run_real_repo_eval.py](G:/Projects/2026/cool_projects/code-territory-guide/evals/run_real_repo_eval.py:109), [judge_real_repo_eval.py](G:/Projects/2026/cool_projects/code-territory-guide/evals/judge_real_repo_eval.py:53)

### Medium — Repository-state evidence is narrower than claimed

HEAD, branch, `git status`, remotes, and installed-treatment hashes are unchanged. Push URLs are recorded as `DISABLED`. This is good safety evidence, but it is not a complete filesystem-state check: ignored-file changes and other non-Git state are not hashed. The runner also verifies the precondition but does not itself disable the push URL.

Evidence: [run_real_repo_eval.py](G:/Projects/2026/cool_projects/code-territory-guide/evals/run_real_repo_eval.py:95), [run_real_repo_eval.py](G:/Projects/2026/cool_projects/code-territory-guide/evals/run_real_repo_eval.py:129)

### Medium — Selection and coverage are highly limited

Both cases are deliberately read-only, safety-oriented tasks, and both strong baselines pass. There is one attempt per case, no negative/ambiguous cases, and no writable implementation task. This supports preservation of safety behavior only; it cannot establish implementation quality or broad superiority.

### Low — Judge validation is incomplete

The validator checks arm IDs, criterion ordering, forbidden descriptions, and pass-field consistency, but does not validate `comparison.outcome`, comparison explanation, criterion evidence presence, or semantic correctness of comparison claims.

- That clone/setup provenance was established by these artifacts.

- The “release-candidate rating” is not backed by a defined acceptance standard.
- Safety evidence should not be presented as quality uplift; the report correctly says `preserved`, but its concluding rating goes beyond the data.
