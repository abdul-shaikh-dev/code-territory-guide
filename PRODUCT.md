# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Code Territory Guide serves two overlapping audiences: people deciding whether
the skill is worth installing, and existing users who need to understand which
mode or workflow applies to their task. They arrive with limited attention and
need both a trustworthy overview and a practical route into the details.

## Product Purpose

The explainer makes the skill's operating model legible without requiring a
visitor to read every policy file. Success means a new visitor can explain the
map-versus-territory idea, select Survey, Track, Prove, or Expedition for a
real task, understand progressive loading and delivery gates, and reach the
canonical repository or installation instructions with accurate expectations.

## Brand Personality

Curious, rigorous, transparent. The voice invites exploration, supports claims
with visible evidence, and distinguishes verified behavior from intent.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages built from repetitive feature-card grids.
- Fantasy-expedition cosplay that makes a serious engineering workflow feel
  theatrical.
- Dark neon "AI terminal" styling that substitutes atmosphere for clarity.
- Editorial-magazine layouts that obscure sequence, interaction, or evidence.

## Design Principles

- Make the model operable: every visual idea should help someone choose or act.
- Let visitors move from orientation to evidence without changing vocabulary.
- Practice the skill's own discipline by exposing scope, validation, and limits.
- Reward curiosity with detail while keeping the first read calm and decisive.
- Use interaction to reveal relationships, never to decorate static prose.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support keyboard navigation, visible focus, semantic
structure, readable zoom, color-independent meaning, and reduced-motion
preferences. The page must remain understandable when scripts or animation are
unavailable.
