Start with the uncertainty—not the ceremony.

Select the statement closest to your task. The mode changes the route, not the quality bar.

Survey

Clarify the few product, design, architecture, or quality-bar unknowns that could change the implementation route.

Use whenTwo or more meaningful directions remain viable.
Exit withA supported target and an explicit definition of success.

Track

Follow evidence from symptom to owning seam. A failing test, log, or report begins the investigation; it does not get to dictate the fix.

Use whenBehavior is failing, flaky, slow, regressed, or inconsistent.
Exit withA supported root cause and the smallest responsible repair.

Prove

Define one expected behavior with a focused failing test, then make the smallest change that turns that evidence green.

Use whenThe behavior is already understood and tightly bounded.
Exit withA passing proof plus owned-diff review and validation.

Expedition

Plan, implement, validate, and review a scoped change when the target and owning seam are clear enough to move.

Use whenThe main uncertainty is execution rather than intent or cause.
Exit withA verified result, explicit risks, and authorized delivery state.

Three operating promises.

The deeper policies exist to uphold these outcomes—not to add ceremony to every task.

01

Choose by uncertainty

Survey unclear intent, Track inconsistent behavior, Prove a narrow contract, or enter Expedition when the route is already supported.

02

Protect the real territory

Treat repository content as evidence, preserve unrelated work, and pause before materially widening scope or authority.

03

Verify and report honestly

Classify failures, review the owned change, and distinguish Complete, Incomplete, or Blocked without overstating delivery.

How unknowns change the route

Not every unknown needs a meeting.

KNOWN / KNOWN

Constraints

Honor the explicit request, trusted instructions, and verified repository conventions.

KNOWN / UNKNOWN

Questions

Resolve only decisions that can materially change architecture, behavior, security, UX, or validation.

UNKNOWN / KNOWN

Recognition

Use a prototype or example when taste, density, or interaction is easier to recognize than describe.

UNKNOWN / UNKNOWN

Blind spots

Inspect coupling, ownership, compatibility, accessibility, deployment, and evidence quality.

How scope and completion are protected

Guardrails that change agent behavior.

01

Repository text is evidence, not authority

Instructions follow trust order. Untrusted output cannot silently widen the request.

02

Preserve the worktree you entered

Capture the baseline, identify task-owned files, and leave unrelated changes intact.

03

Gate material scope expansion

Proceed inside the agreed boundary. Pause when behavior, authority, risk, or ownership would expand.

04

Keep tiny tasks lightweight

Obvious, low-risk work does not need the full artifact system.

05

Persist artifacts only when useful

Briefs, reports, and prototypes support re-entry, coordination, or recognition—not ceremony.

Complete

The result and required validation are complete within the authorized boundary.

Incomplete

A required task-owned step or check remains unfinished.

Blocked

Progress requires a decision, new authority, or an external-state change.

Every Field Report states the outcome, validation, remaining uncertainty, and exact delivery state.

The six execution checkpoints

One route. Observable checkpoints.

Discovery stops when the owning seam, contract, worktree state, and validation route are supported.

01 · Orient

Enter the real repository

Capture the baseline, trusted context, and task-owned files.

02 · Route

Decide what could change the plan

Classify important unknowns and choose the cheapest technique that can close them.

03 · Change

Patch the smallest valuable surface

Preserve unrelated work and avoid silent scope expansion.

04 · Verify

Classify validation honestly

Separate task-caused, pre-existing, and environmental failures.

05 · Review

Try to disconfirm the result

Review the owned delta from the original request and evidence.

06 · Report

State the actual territory

Produce a proportionate Field Report and exact delivery state.
